Additional payments for teaching: eligibility and payment details

We give additional payments to early career teachers who are teaching certain subjects. These payments are to encourage those teachers to stay in the profession.

We also give payments to early career teachers teaching certain subjects in particular schools. These payments are because the school is part of the government levelling up scheme.

Check the rules for who can claim and how payments are made.

Additional payments

Additional payments are available to early career teachers who qualified in and now teach either chemistry, computing, foreign languages, mathematics and physics.

You may be eligible for an early-career payment, if you teach chemistry, foreign languages, mathematics or physics in an eligible school.

You may be able to apply for a levelling up premium payment if you teach chemistry, computing, mathematics or physics in an eligible school.

If you are eligible for both payments, you will only be able to claim one of these payments this year.
